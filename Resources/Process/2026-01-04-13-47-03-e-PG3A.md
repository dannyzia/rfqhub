Guidance Notes on the Use of
The Standard Tender Document

These guidance notes have been prepared by the BPPA to assist the Procuring Entity in the preparation
of  tender  document  using  the  Standard  Tender  Document  (STD),  for  the  procurement  of  Goods  and
related services. The Procuring Entity should also refer to the Public Procurement Act 2006(Act No 24 of
2006)  and  the  Public  Procurement  Rules,  2025,  issued  to  supplement  the  Act,  available  on  BPPA’s
website  www.bppa.gov.bd/  including  amendments  thereto.  All  concerned  are  advised  to  refer  to  the
aforementioned Act and Rules while participating in any Tendering process.

STD (e-PG3A) applies when a Purchaser (the Procuring Entity) wishes to select a Tenderer (a Supplier)
for the supply of Goods and related services through a Framework arrangement under Open Tendering
Method or Limited Tendering Method (OTM/LTM) for which the Contract award is being determined on
the basis of the lowest evaluated responsive Tender.

This STD also refers to such a Framework Agreement, where a Procuring Entity (Purchaser) makes an
agreement for a definite period with a single supplier for delivery of fixed or estimated quantity of goods
and accomplishment of related services where actual deliveries are performed through several Call-off
contracts.

STD (e-PG3A) is based upon internationally acceptable model formats, which have been adapted to suit
the particular needs of procurement within Bangladesh.

The Procuring Entity addresses its specific needs through the information provided in the Tender Data
Sheet (TDS) and the Particular Conditions of Agreement (PCA) as well as in the detailed requirements of
the procurement in the Schedule of Requirements, the Technical Specifications and/or the Drawings.

Guidance notes in brackets and italics are provided for both the Procuring Entity and the Tenderers and,
the Procuring Entity should carefully decide what notes need to remain and what other guidance notes
might be required to assist the Tenderers in preparing its Tender Submission so as to minimise an inept
Tendering process.

STD  (e-PG3A),  provides  all  the  information  that  a  Tenderer  needs  in  order  to  prepare  and  submit  a
Tender. This should provide a sound basis on which the Procuring Entity can fairly, transparently and
accurately carry out a Tender evaluation process on the Tenders submitted by the Tenderers.

STD  (e-PG3A)  has  6  Sections,  of  which  Section  1  (Instructions  to  Tenderers)  and  Section  3  (General
Conditions of Contract) shall not be altered or modified under any circumstances.
The following briefly describes the Sections of the STD (e-PG3A) and how the Procuring Entity should
use these when preparing a particular Tender Document.

Scope and Type of Contract
The STD is suitable for any scope of intended agreement where the goods and services are pre-defined
in the context of both quality (specs) and quantity and the overall period of delivery timeline are fixed
enabling a total agreement amount to be calculated from total quantities of goods to be delivered through
several Call-off orders. Contracts and Payments shall be done against each of the Call-Off orders made
under the Framework Agreement.
